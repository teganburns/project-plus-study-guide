    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 1</p>
    <h2>Which of the following might be contained in the Project Charter? Choose 3.</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>Schedule milestones</li>
      <li>High level requirements</li>
      <li>A list of key stakeholders</li>
      <li>The Scope Statement</li>
      <li>Detailed risk analysis</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: A, B, and C.</strong> The project charter contains high-level initiation information, including schedule milestones, high-level requirements, and key stakeholders.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Schedule milestones:</strong> Major high-level schedule points that may be included in the charter.</li>
        <li><strong>High level requirements:</strong> Early requirements that describe what the project needs to achieve without becoming a detailed requirements document.</li>
        <li><strong>Key stakeholders:</strong> Important people or groups connected to the project, often identified during initiation.</li>
        <li><strong>Scope Statement:</strong> A more detailed planning document, not usually the charter itself.</li>
        <li><strong>Detailed risk analysis:</strong> A deeper planning activity. The charter may include overall project risk, but not a full detailed risk analysis.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 2</p>
    <h2>Which of the following might be examples of stakeholders? Choose all that apply.</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>Shareholder</li>
      <li>Other managers</li>
      <li>Customer</li>
      <li>User</li>
      <li>Sponsor</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: A, B, C, D, and E.</strong> A stakeholder is any person, group, or organization that may affect the project or be affected by the project.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Shareholder:</strong> May care about business value, financial impact, or strategic outcomes.</li>
        <li><strong>Other managers:</strong> May provide resources, approve work, or be affected by project changes.</li>
        <li><strong>Customer:</strong> Receives or pays for the product, service, or result.</li>
        <li><strong>User:</strong> Uses the product, service, or result created by the project.</li>
        <li><strong>Sponsor:</strong> Authorizes, champions, or funds the project and may approve key decisions.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 3</p>
    <h2>One of the stakeholders has ultimate responsibility and sign off for a deliverable in the project. Which level of participation should you give them in the RACI matrix?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>Responsible</li>
      <li>Informed</li>
      <li>Consulted</li>
      <li>Accountable</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: D.</strong> Accountable is the RACI role with ultimate ownership, approval, or sign off for the deliverable.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Responsible:</strong> Does the work.</li>
        <li><strong>Informed:</strong> Needs to be kept in the loop.</li>
        <li><strong>Consulted:</strong> Provides information, input, or expertise.</li>
        <li><strong>Accountable:</strong> Approves or signs off the work. There should be only one Accountable person per item, but they may also be Responsible.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 4</p>
    <h2>Every scrap of paper, every post-it note, every phone call transcript should be treated as a record in our project. True or false?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>True</li>
      <li>False</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: B.</strong> Not every piece of written information is a record. A document can be working information that may still change, while a record is retained evidence of something that happened, was approved, was completed, or must be preserved under policy.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Document:</strong> Any written information, including working notes, drafts, templates, or in-progress materials.</li>
        <li><strong>Record:</strong> Final or retained evidence, such as an approved charter, signed approval, final acceptance, or completed change record.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 5</p>
    <h2>Which is the best description of a Product Roadmap?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>Detailed activity time estimates</li>
      <li>High level time frames for major themes of the product</li>
      <li>The Agile version of the Cost Baseline</li>
      <li>High level outline of the business purpose of the product</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: B.</strong> A Product Roadmap provides high-level time frames for major themes of the product. It is a strategic planning tool, not a detailed schedule.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Detailed activity time estimates:</strong> More like schedule planning detail, not a product roadmap.</li>
        <li><strong>High level time frames for major themes:</strong> The best description of a product roadmap.</li>
        <li><strong>Agile version of the Cost Baseline:</strong> Not correct. A cost baseline is about approved budget expectations.</li>
        <li><strong>High level outline of the business purpose:</strong> This describes the Product Vision, not the Product Roadmap.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 6</p>
    <h2>Which of the following are goals of the kickoff meeting? Choose 3.</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>Communicate project goals</li>
      <li>Make introductions</li>
      <li>Brainstorm task durations</li>
      <li>Set expectations and clarify roles</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: A, B, and D.</strong> The kickoff meeting is used to align the team and sponsor around the project purpose, people, expectations, roles, and next steps.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Communicate project goals:</strong> A core kickoff purpose so everyone understands what the project is trying to achieve.</li>
        <li><strong>Make introductions:</strong> Helps the project team, sponsor, and key stakeholders know who is involved.</li>
        <li><strong>Brainstorm task durations:</strong> More detailed schedule planning work, not a primary kickoff meeting goal.</li>
        <li><strong>Set expectations and clarify roles:</strong> A core kickoff purpose so people understand responsibilities, working norms, and what happens next.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 7</p>
    <h2>Which of the following best describes the likely role of the Project Sponsor in the creation of the Project Charter?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>The Sponsor creates the charter, and passes to Stakeholders for approval</li>
      <li>The Sponsor will not see the charter until after the kickoff meeting</li>
      <li>The Sponsor has no interest in the charter</li>
      <li>The Project Manager might create the charter, but the Sponsor approves it</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: D.</strong> The Project Manager may help draft or prepare the charter, but the project sponsor approves it and authorizes the project to begin.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Sponsor creates and stakeholders approve:</strong> Not the best answer. Stakeholders may provide input, but the sponsor is the key approval authority for the charter.</li>
        <li><strong>Sponsor does not see it until after kickoff:</strong> Not correct. The charter should be approved before kickoff because it authorizes the project.</li>
        <li><strong>Sponsor has no interest:</strong> Not correct. The sponsor is central to authorizing and supporting the project.</li>
        <li><strong>PM may create, sponsor approves:</strong> Best answer. The sponsor approves or issues the charter, even if the PM helps write it.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 8</p>
    <h2>Why do we need to assess stakeholders? Choose 2.</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>To identify what their influence on the project might be</li>
      <li>To spot personality clashes early on</li>
      <li>To help us prioritise relationships</li>
      <li>To remove those who might be resistant to the project</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: A and C.</strong> Stakeholder assessment helps the project team understand influence, interest, expectations, and relationship priority so communication and engagement can be planned intelligently.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Identify influence:</strong> Correct. Knowing stakeholder influence helps the team understand who can affect decisions, resources, approval, or adoption.</li>
        <li><strong>Spot personality clashes:</strong> Not the main purpose. Conflict may happen, but stakeholder assessment is not primarily a personality-screening exercise.</li>
        <li><strong>Prioritise relationships:</strong> Correct. Stakeholders with high influence, high interest, or critical expectations may need closer engagement.</li>
        <li><strong>Remove resistant stakeholders:</strong> Not correct. Resistant stakeholders should be understood and managed, not simply removed.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 9</p>
    <h2>What does RACI stand for?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>Reported, Assessed, Consulted, Informed</li>
      <li>Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed</li>
      <li>Required, Answerable, Controlled, Indicated</li>
      <li>Required, Accountable, Contracted, Idle</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: B.</strong> RACI stands for Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Responsible:</strong> Does the work. This is the person or role carrying out the task or deliverable.</li>
        <li><strong>Accountable:</strong> Approves or signs off the work. There should be only one Accountable person per item, and that person can also be Responsible.</li>
        <li><strong>Consulted:</strong> Provides input, information, or expertise before the work or decision is finalized.</li>
        <li><strong>Informed:</strong> Needs to be kept in the loop about progress, decisions, or outcomes.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 10</p>
    <h2>Why is it important to include a definition of a record in our Records Management Plan?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>So that accountable practices can be applied</li>
      <li>To avoid fines through deleting sensitive information</li>
      <li>So that team members know what documents they must keep</li>
      <li>In order to avoid running out of cloud storage</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: C.</strong> A Records Management Plan should define what counts as a record so team members know which documents must be kept and managed according to retention rules.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Accountable practices:</strong> Clear practices matter, but this option is too vague. The key purpose is identifying what must be retained.</li>
        <li><strong>Deleting sensitive information:</strong> Records management may include disposal rules, but the point is not simply to delete sensitive information to avoid fines.</li>
        <li><strong>Documents they must keep:</strong> Correct. Defining a record tells the team what project information needs to be retained as evidence.</li>
        <li><strong>Cloud storage:</strong> Storage limits may be operationally relevant, but they are not the main reason for defining records in a Records Management Plan.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 11</p>
    <h2>Which is the best definition of a Product Roadmap?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>An outline of general time frames for major themes of the product</li>
      <li>A milestone list of costs</li>
      <li>The schedule baseline</li>
      <li>A detailed release burndown chart</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: A.</strong> A Product Roadmap outlines general time frames for major themes of the product. It is a strategic planning tool that gives direction without locking every detail up front.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>General time frames for major themes:</strong> Correct. This is the core purpose of a product roadmap.</li>
        <li><strong>Milestone list of costs:</strong> Not correct. Costs belong to budget planning, not the product roadmap definition.</li>
        <li><strong>Schedule baseline:</strong> Not correct. A schedule baseline is an approved schedule plan used for tracking project performance.</li>
        <li><strong>Release burndown chart:</strong> Not correct. A burndown chart tracks remaining work over time; it is more detailed than a roadmap.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
  </article>

  <article class="quiz-card">
    <p class="quiz-meta">Chapter 4 Question 12</p>
    <h2>Once the Project Charter has been approved, what is the next thing the Project Manager might do?</h2>

    <ol class="quiz-options" type="A">
      <li>Prepare the Project Schedule</li>
      <li>Distribute the Business Case to all stakeholders</li>
      <li>Sign off the Cost Baseline</li>
      <li>Conduct the Project Kickoff Meeting</li>
    </ol>

    <details class="quiz-answer">
      <summary>Show answer</summary>
      <p><strong>Answer: D.</strong> Once the Project Charter is approved, the project is authorized to begin, so the Project Manager may conduct the kickoff meeting to align the team and sponsor around purpose, expectations, roles, and next steps.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Prepare the Project Schedule:</strong> Important planning work, but usually comes after initiation alignment and more detailed planning.</li>
        <li><strong>Distribute the Business Case:</strong> The business case supports the decision to proceed; it normally comes before charter approval.</li>
        <li><strong>Sign off the Cost Baseline:</strong> A planning/baseline activity, not the immediate next initiation step after charter approval.</li>
        <li><strong>Conduct the Project Kickoff Meeting:</strong> Best answer. The kickoff helps introduce people, communicate goals, set expectations, clarify roles, and answer questions.</li>
      </ul>
    </details>
